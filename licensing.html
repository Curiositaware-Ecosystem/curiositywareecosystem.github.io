Licensing in the Curiosityware Ecosystem

The **Curiosityware Ecosystem** is committed to fostering open-source innovation, aiming to empower developers and creators worldwide to utilize our projects as widely as possible. In this spirit, every project published on our platform is released under a permissive open-source license best suited to its domain, ensuring both freedom of use and proper attribution.

---

Software Projects: MIT License

All software projects released under the **Curiosityware Ecosystem** are governed by the MIT License. This highly permissive license ensures that:

  • **Freedom of Use:** Anyone can freely use, copy, modify, and distribute the software for any purpose, including commercial applications.
  • **Minimal Restrictions:** The only significant requirement is that the MIT license text and the copyright notice must be included in all copies or substantial portions of the software.

Copyright Information Under the MIT License:

In the LICENSE file of our software projects, and at the beginning of the source code files, the following copyright notice is included:

Copyright (c) 2025 Dominik István Bálló, Curiosityware Ecosystem

This guarantees that the project's origin and creators receive due recognition, while developers enjoy complete freedom in utilizing our software.

---

Hardware Projects: CERN Open Hardware Licence v2 - Permissive (CERN-OHL-P-2.0)

Hardware projects within the **Curiosityware Ecosystem**, including all HDL (Hardware Description Language) based designs, are protected under the CERN Open Hardware Licence Version 2 - Permissive (CERN-OHL-P-2.0). This license is specifically optimized for open hardware design and provides the following:

  • **Maximum Flexibility:** Designs can be freely used, copied, modified, and distributed, including for commercial manufacturing and sale.
  • **Derivative Works:** Users are permitted to develop their own products or solutions based on our designs, even if they choose to keep their derivative works proprietary, without being obligated to release their designs openly.
  • **Fair Attribution:** The license requires that the original creator and source (the Curiosityware Ecosystem) be clearly attributed in all derivative works and distributions.

Copyright Information Under the CERN-OHL-P-2.0 License:

In the LICENSE or NOTICE file of our hardware projects, and within the HDL source files, the following copyright notice will be found:

Copyright (c) 2025 Dominik István Bálló, Curiosityware Ecosystem

This license ensures that our hardware innovations can spread widely and inspire further development, while the original creator and the **Curiosityware Ecosystem** receive appropriate acknowledgment.

Our choice of both the MIT and CERN-OHL-P-2.0 licenses reflects our commitment to enabling the **Curiosityware Ecosystem** projects to foster technological advancement, learning, and commercial innovation in the freest possible manner.
